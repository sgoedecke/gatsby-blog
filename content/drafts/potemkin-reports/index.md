Large organizations function via a tree of nested reports. Individual contributors give updates to their managers, who summarize and combine[^1] those into team-level updates for their managers, and so on all the way to the top.

This serves two purposes. The obvious purpose is _legibility_: managers and executives can find out what is happening in the organization simply by reading the relevant report[^2]. A less-obvious but equally important purpose is that it forces people to build a mental model of what's going on in their local area. Spending the time to write a status report forces you to wrap your head around each detail, like how the best way to understand an idea is to try to explain it to someone else.
